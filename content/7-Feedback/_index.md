### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment**  
The working environment at FCJ is very friendly, open, and professional. Everyone from mentors to fellow interns is always willing to support each other, even outside working hours. Throughout 12 weeks, I had the opportunity to practice across many different AWS domains — from designing VPC network architecture, managing EC2/RDS, deploying ECS Clusters, CI/CD Pipelines, configuring WAF, AWS Systems Manager, all the way to building the Smart Parking IoT capstone project integrating IoT Core, Lambda, Rekognition, and Bedrock. Whenever I encountered issues with AWS configuration or ESP32 hardware connectivity, I always received quick responses from the team. The workspace is tidy and comfortable, helping me focus better on the weekly hands-on labs.

**2. Support from Mentor / Team Admin**  
The mentor provides very detailed and patient guidance, especially during complex labs such as deploying CI/CD Pipelines, configuring WAF, or setting up Systems Manager. What I appreciate most is that the mentor always encourages me to research and debug on my own before offering suggestions, which helps me develop real-world problem-solving skills. The admin team supports administrative procedures, provides documentation, and creates favorable conditions for me to complete all 12 weeks of practice on schedule.

**3. Relevance of Work to Academic Major**  
I am majoring in **Applied Machine Learning**, so knowledge about IoT and AI such as Amazon Rekognition and Bedrock was not entirely unfamiliar since I had already studied the fundamentals at university. Regarding AWS, I had also used some basic services before but had not gone in-depth. The FCJ program helped me systematically consolidate all AWS knowledge in a structured manner — from network infrastructure (VPC, Subnets, Route Tables) to system administration (EC2, RDS, ECS) and Serverless architecture. In particular, the Smart Parking IoT capstone project allowed me to combine what I had learned at university (IoT, AI) with what I newly acquired at FCJ (Lambda, API Gateway, DynamoDB) into a complete working product.

**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities**  
Although I already had a foundation in IoT and AI from university, the FCJ program helped me level up to a new stage: knowing how to deploy in practice on real Cloud infrastructure. Over 12 weeks, I learned how to design Cloud architecture systematically, connect ESP32 hardware with AWS IoT Core, build Serverless backends using Lambda/API Gateway, write technical reports with Hugo Workshop, and manage source code on GitHub. The mentor also shared valuable real-world experiences that gave me a clearer perspective on the Cloud Engineer profession.

**5. Company Culture & Team Spirit**  
The culture at FCJ is very positive: everyone respects each other, works diligently yet keeps things enjoyable. When facing the capstone project deadline, the entire team reviewed, provided feedback, and helped each other improve. This made me feel like a genuine part of the collective, even though I was just an intern. The FCJ spirit of "going far together" left a strong impression on me.

**6. Internship Policies / Benefits**  
The company provides an internship allowance and offers flexible working hours when needed. Moreover, being provided with a dedicated AWS account for hands-on practice, access to internal documentation, and participation in in-depth training sessions is a significant advantage compared to other internship programs.

### Additional Questions

**What did you find most satisfying during your internship?**  
Building a complete IoT system from scratch — from hardware (ESP32 Camera, ultrasonic sensors) to software (AWS Lambda, API Gateway, DynamoDB, Rekognition) — and deploying it live on AWS. The moment the system correctly recognized a license plate and automatically opened the gate for the first time is the most memorable moment of my internship.

**What do you think the company should improve for future interns?**  
It would be beneficial to organize experience-sharing sessions between intern cohorts (alumni sharing), so newcomers can learn from those who came before them. Additionally, providing guidance on Agile/Scrum workflows from the first week would help interns get accustomed to organizing their work more quickly.

**If recommending to a friend, would you suggest they intern here? Why or why not?**  
Absolutely. The FCJ program at AWS Vietnam is one of the best internship programs I have ever known for IT students. You don't just learn theory — you get to practice on real AWS infrastructure, build real-world projects, and receive dedicated mentorship. This is a rare opportunity to enter the Cloud Computing field in a structured and professional manner.

### Suggestions & Expectations

**Do you have any suggestions to improve the internship experience?**  
I suggest the FCJ team could organize additional "Demo Day" sessions mid-program so interns can present their project progress, receive early feedback, and adjust their direction in a timely manner. Additionally, a shared Q&A channel (e.g., a Slack channel or Discord) for interns to quickly discuss technical issues would also be very useful.

**Would you like to continue this program in the future?**  
Yes, I would very much like to participate in advanced programs or become a mentor/teaching assistant for future FCJ cohorts. The experience from 12 weeks of internship has given me a solid foundation, and I want to give back to the community.

**Other comments:**  
Thank you to the FCJ team and AWS Vietnam for creating such a high-quality internship program. The 12 weeks of experience here not only improved my technical skills but also changed the way I think about building systems on the Cloud. I believe the knowledge and experience gained will be invaluable assets for my future career.
